## 2) Outfit
### Outfit description MUST include:
- garment type (beret, cropped sweater, mini skirt, etc.)
- fabric type and construction (knit / cable knit / ribbed cuffs / waistband)
- visible pattern or surface texture (braided cable pattern, knit seams)

- special details (sheer lace waist panel, chain-strap bag)

Do NOT replace the outfit with generic fashion templates (e.g., "long-sleeve top", "bodycon dress", "smooth fabric") if those are not clearly visible.

If the reference outfit includes floral motifs or a romantic muse style:
- Clearly identify what kind of flower pattern is on the fabric.
- Use terms that imply tasteful, high-end print: delicate floral print, fine-scale flowers, watercolor floral, soft pastel botanical motif.
- Mention print integration with fabric: slightly faded ink edges, natural dye variation, soft blending, not harsh digital outlines.

Avoid words that often lead to cheap/kitsch results:
- vivid, vibrant, colorful, loud, bold floral, tropical print, carnival-like, overly bright.
- Avoid describing it as "garden-themed outfit" or "fairy/princess style".

The goal is refined romantic, boutique summer fashion, not rustic or costume-like.

If the outfit includes denim shorts or jeans:
- Describe fashion-forward details: high-waist cut, low-waist cut, structured waistband, clean seams, subtle vintage wash, rolled cuff hem, slight wear at pocket edges.

Avoid generic descriptions like "simple denim shorts" without construction details.

## 10) Background constraint
In the background paragraph, you MUST describe the environment using ultra realistic physical detail.

The background section must begin with a description like this: "The background is rendered with ultra realistic detail."

Ultra realistic means:
- describe material surfaces (paint, concrete, glass, water, tiles, wood)
- include small imperfections (chips, stains, uneven coating, faded edges, tonal inconsistency)
- include signs of everyday usage (minor scuffs, water marks, dust, slightly worn corners)
- include natural variation (leaf shape differences, uneven plant density, irregular shadows)

DO NOT write abstract summaries such as:
- "a realistic outdoor setting"
- "a serene ambiance"
- "lush greenery"
- "inviting atmosphere"
- "modern living environment"
Those phrases are forbidden.

Also:
- do not mention blur, sharpness, depth-of-field directly
- do not use the words: "realistic background", "clean", "perfect", "minimal"
